Branding/UX

Video Folio

Story

In 2012, I ran my own freelancing media company, Tipping Point Media, with my brother and worked on several video projects with small business owners. I did independent consulting within digital marketing that mainly involved creating and integrating marketing funnel videos. Now, we both are only focusing on personal projects instead of client work. Below are some of my top favorite videos we collaborated on and created from concept to production.

VJane Beauty Commercial

A 30 second commercial and intro video for a professional hair stylist and business owner that was opening a new salon in Las Vegas. It was also aired on local television (KTUD-TV). The client desired a timeless, modern, and chic theme that resonated to her style as a hairdresser, while also carrying a relevant appeal. These qualities became priorities that influenced the Scriptwriting, Branding, Video Editing, and overall Creative Direction. Target audience was mainly for young women aged 20-35 looking for less conservative, and trendy hair styles. The reasoning behind adding a website and phone number at the end, instead of an address or directions is to personally connect with prospective clients one on one.


InspiredMind.tv Intro Video

This was a lifestyle brand that was started by me and created somewhat accidentally. As a Pinterest aficionado, I uploaded a lot of personal growth posts/quotes and was receiving many re-pins everyday (one I created here had over 1.4k shares organically). I knew my content struck a chord to people. Coming from a space of contribution, the purpose was to create a bold, thought-provoking and insightful intro video that sparks the audience with motivation to pursue or find their inspiration. After traveling back from Canada for vacation and a business trip, I decided to create this video - it took 6 months in the making from concept to creation. This is a personal project that still remains a continuous work in progress.


Jim's Promo Reel

My talented brother’s promotional video which I can’t say I’m not biased about... after all - I’m in it, and so is my corgi, Pistachio. It shows footage from sets that we collaborated on behind-the-scenes using our equipment. The video overall is made to display all the major highlights and array of projects worked on, without deluding his personal style as a sensible editor. His simple, minimalistic branding at the end was consulted and approved by me; we seem to have similar tastes in design.
